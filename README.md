## Research

### Pan-Cancer Germline Predisposition Analysis

Analyzed inherited pathogenic variants across 44 cancer predisposition genes and 9 cancer types in 61,044 participants from the NIH All of Us Research Program. Replicated 95% (20/21) of Huang et al.'s (*Cell*, 2018) TCGA findings in an independent, ancestrally diverse cohort and discovered 34 novel gene-cancer associations. Revealed ancestry-specific predisposition patterns invisible in TCGA's European-dominant data — including 21× BRCA2 enrichment in Black/AA breast cancer vs 10.9× in White, and 57.7× APC enrichment in Hispanic colorectal cancer vs 8.5× in White.

Repo: [pan-cancer-germline-predisposition](https://github.com/mohibul-07/Pan-Cancer-Germline-Predisposition)
Dashboard: [germline-predisposition.vercel.app](https://germline-predisposition.vercel.app)

### Colorectal Cancer Risk Prediction Using Machine Learning

Built an XGBoost model integrating 31 clinical, socioeconomic, and germline genomic features to predict colorectal cancer risk. Engineered Lynch syndrome (14× enrichment) and APC (27× enrichment, p=0.000012) variant features from whole genome sequencing data. Deployed as a full-stack clinical decision support tool with real-time SHAP explanations and PDF report export.

Model: XGBoost v2 · AUC 0.8979 · 12,248 patients
Repo: [colorectal-cancer-risk-prediction](https://github.com/mohibul-07/colorectal-cancer-risk-prediction)
Live app: [colorectal-cancer-risk-prediction.vercel.app](https://colorectal-cancer-risk-prediction.vercel.app)

Research conducted under Professor Zeynep Akcay Ozkan through the CUNY Research Scholars Program. Presented at CURLI (April 2026).
